How to find the voicebank you need

This webpage is designed to easily download any KOI-m voicebank. To find a specific voicebank just right for your needs, you can describe features like language (Japanese, English...), voicebank type (CV, VCV...) or append name (Dark, Power...) to the "Quick Search"-tool. If you need further information on the voicebanks, just go through the descriptions attatched below.

If you are concerned some of the work you plan to do with her might be not appropiate to me, feel free to look up the Terms of Use or simply contact me.

Have fun and don't forget to send me your amazing work!

Outdated Voicebanks:

Japanese Voicebanks:


Japanese CV -Dolly-:

A multi-expression bank, which uses the same reclist as in the first act but with extra files. The two expressions are: Sobbing (S) and Howling (H). With the 820 recorded samples of her, Engrishing gets a little more easy. She uses both, Hiragana and Romanized filenames.


Japanese VCV -Dolly-:

A multi-expression bank, which uses the same reclist as in the first act but in two expressions. The two expressions are: Sobbing (S) and Howling (H), just like for the CV equivalent. She uses both, Hiragana and Romanized filenames as well.


Japanese VCV -Natural-:

Pentapitch Japanese VCV voicebank, which sounds very similar to my actual singing voice and is intended to do so. The recorded pitches are: B3, D4, F4, G#4, B4. She uses both, Hiragana and Romanized filenames.


Japanese VCV -Childish-:

Tripitch Japanese VCV voicebank, which is intended to sound rather cute and childish. I recorded this by raising my soft palate. The recorded pitches are: B3, F4, B4. She uses both, Hiragana and Romanized filenames.


Japanese VCV -Dark-:

Tetrapitch Japanese VCV voicebank, which is intended to sound voluminous, mature and deep - a little bit like an opera voice. The recorded pitches are: AB, C4, E4, A4. She uses both, Hiragana and Romanized filenames. I can only recommend using this bank.


Japanese VCV -Power-:

Tetrapitch Japanese VCV voicebank, which is intended to sound strong and powerful, but not voluminous, like the Dark append. C4 pitch is a bit buggy, so I usually don't include it.


Korean Voicebanks:


Korean CVVC -Porcelain-:

Features a custom short reclist to sing in Korean, which probably isn't the best because I have no idea how Korean works. Still it's super fast to record and oto with only around 100 sample in it. The bank used to be called CVC.


Russian Voicebanks:


Russian CVC -Porcelain-:

Features a usual CVC reclist to sing in Russian. This bank is recorded in a pretty natural singing voice, since it is intended to resemble my natural singing voice.


French Voicebanks:


French CV -Porcelain-:

Only a small bank I made in order to test out NiaoNIAO Synth, configured on both, UTAU and NiaoNIAO. Adds breaths and glottal steps, custom reclist. The bank features a natural recording voice, as I usually do for -Porcelain- banks. Some sounds are missing here and there, rather use the CVVC bank.


French CVVC -Porcelain-:

A clear natural type bank which is able to sing in French. It features a reclist and alias system I created myself which is intended to work similar to French spelling.


English Voicebanks:


English/Multilingual CVVC -Bluemoon-:

A multilingual voicebank with a soft and cute tone. It works with many different European languages such as English, French, German and more.


English VCCV -Porcelain-:

A monopitch English bank recorded with the original CZlist. Works with most of the English USTs and can sing in different genres.
